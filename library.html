The Rains Fallen - Dawnlight Chronicle Book 1

As the prophet Kibal once said, "The rains began to fall, and humanity ran for cover."

For centuries the people of the island nations of Oyldorch have lived at the mercy of extreme weather conditions. Although the Sacred Texts tell of a time when the skies were clear, the origins of the incessant precipitation are unknown.
The story begins with Ceann, a young man from the island Dochas, working in the underground wheat fields alongside his friends Ada and Triu. Like any other day, they find their work monotonous but important, and enjoy their time together sharing stories and gossip. But this day is different. As they are nearing the end of their shift a massive earthquake shakes the island.
Although at first they are relieved that none of the crop caves collapsed, they are horrified when they return to their village - nestled beneath the enormous cliffs of Cosantoir - and find it buried beneath the collapsed cliffs.
With almost everyone they've ever known lost to the calamity, the three friends decide to make their way to the island capital - the citadel Sciath - to inform the Island Steward of what has happened.
Thus they leave their at times boring and uncomfortable - but always familiar - lives behind them. And set out to lands foreign to them; where uncertainty and danger await them.

Little do they know, their odyssey will be more challenging than they thought. And they will discover along the way that they possess ancient powers lost to memory which only through intense ordeal come to light.

The Enemies Made - Dawnlight Chronicle Book 2

"Ever has it been known that friendship knows not its own depth until the hour of separation"
- Unknown

Upon reaching their destination at the end of the previous novel only to find it in ruins, the three friends make a decision. To each take upon themselves a separate task. Triu will remain in the valley of Sciath to organise a new settlement for the survivors of the disaster, with the help of the mysterious elder Ciallmar. Who will help him understand and master his newfound powers of Protection.
Ada will investigate the abnormal natural disasters as she believes their origin to be unnatural. She will begin her inquiry at the Temple of Eagna where the ancient Wisdoms are said to dwell. There she will learn about her power of Illusion.
And Ceann will travel to the central island Rikkios to consult with the great Queen Banria about what to do next. During his journey he will discover the full extent of his power of Gravitation.
In addition to our three protagonists, we will follow the solitary wanderer Tubaiste and his mysterious plan to bring the ancient Prophecies of Calamity to fruition. Thinking only he has inherited from the powers of antiquity, he will seek to bring all of humanity to its knees before him using his power of Domination.
As our heroes stray from the path they thought they would follow, the world grows larger before them. Exciting and belittling, as exhilirating as is it dangerous. They can't know for certain what awaits them; but they can be sure they will have changed by their next meeting.

Though the three friends are driven by purpose in their respective missions, they will find their paths burdensome. And discover what they took for granted when they were together.

The Lives Lost - Dawnlight Chronicle Book 3

"Fear will spread through humanity,
doom beating an otherwordly drum,
despite hope sprouting from old and young,
inexorable will prove domination's calamity."
- excerpt from the Prophecies of Calamity.

At the start of the third novel we follow Ceann returning to Sciath alongside the General Laidir and a battallion of the Queen's Elites, ready to escort Triu and the survivors back to the safety of Rikkios.
Tubaiste has infiltrated the settlement of New Sciath under the guise of a priest warning of forthcoming doom much worse than what humanity has experienced thus far. In reality he is poisoning the minds of the weak-willed and discontent, gaining an ever larger following.
Meanwhile, Ada returns from her journey in the previous novel with a warning of her own: the previous natural disasters are only the beginning of a more onerous course of events.
Reunited once more, the three friends will work together to get to the bottom of the increasing conflict among the surviving islanders and face their hitherto unknown foes. They hope to unravel these mysteries soon; before any more lives are lost in vain.
Will our heroes prevail in saving the lives of the innocent? Or will Tubaiste and his mysterious masters manage to spread dread and destruction throughout Oyldorch?

"The choice to fight arrogance with humility, domination with resistance, and fear with hope might save us all"
- Ciallmar to Triu.
